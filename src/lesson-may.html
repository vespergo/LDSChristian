Wilderness, Joshua, and Judges — Be Strong and Courageous

April 27 – May 31  ·  Exodus 35–40; Leviticus; Numbers; Deuteronomy; Joshua; Judges

The Stories This Month

The Tabernacle: While in the wilderness, God asked Israel to build Him a beautiful tent — the Tabernacle — so He could dwell among them. Every detail mattered: the golden lampstand, the ark of the covenant, the curtains, the altar. God's instructions filled chapters, because worshipping God is worth doing carefully and beautifully.

Forty Years in the Wilderness: When Israel reached the border of Canaan and sent spies to scout it, ten spies came back afraid: "The people are giants! We can't do it!" Two spies — Joshua and Caleb — said the opposite: "The Lord is with us! Fear not!" Israel believed the fearful ten, and as a consequence spent forty years wandering in the desert, until that entire fearful generation was gone. Moses himself, because of one disobedient moment, was not permitted to enter. He died on a mountain looking down at the Promised Land.

Joshua and Jericho: Joshua led Israel across the Jordan River and up to the walls of Jericho — an enormous fortified city. God's battle plan was unusual: march around the walls once a day for six days, then seven times on the seventh day, then blow the trumpets and shout. They obeyed. The walls fell flat. Faith in God's instructions, even when they look strange, is the lesson here.

The Judges: After Joshua, Israel kept forgetting God. They'd fall away, face trouble, cry out to God, and He would raise up a judge to deliver them — Deborah, Gideon, Samson. This cycle repeated over and over. Their story is a mirror of our own lives: when we drift from God, things go wrong; when we return, He is there.

"Be strong and courageous. Do not be afraid; do not be discouraged, for the Lord your God will be with you wherever you go." — Joshua 1:9

Let's Talk About It

  • The ten fearful spies focused on the giants; Joshua and Caleb focused on God. When you face something scary, what do you tend to focus on?
  • Moses spent his life leading Israel toward a land he never got to enter. Does that seem unfair to you? What can we learn from Moses's faithfulness anyway?
  • God told Joshua to march around Jericho's walls for seven days. Would that plan have made sense to you? Why do you think God sometimes asks us to do things that seem unusual?
  • The Judges' cycle — forget God, get into trouble, cry out, be rescued, forget again — sounds like a bad pattern. Do you see that pattern in your own life sometimes?
  • Deborah was a female judge and prophet — a leader in ancient Israel. What does that tell you about how God sees people?

Key Scriptures

Deuteronomy 6:5

"And thou shalt love the Lord thy God with all thine heart, and with all thy soul, and with all thy might."

Joshua 1:9

"Have not I commanded thee? Be strong and courageous; be not afraid, neither be thou dismayed: for the Lord thy God is with thee whithersoever thou goest."

Joshua 24:15

"Choose you this day whom ye will serve... but as for me and my house, we will serve the Lord."

✝ How This Points to Jesus Christ

In the wilderness, God provided manna — bread from heaven — every morning to feed Israel. Jesus said, "I am the bread of life" (John 6:35). He is the true manna. The water that Moses struck from the rock in the desert fed an entire nation; Paul wrote that "that Rock was Christ" (1 Corinthians 10:4). The name "Joshua" in Hebrew is the same name as "Jesus" in Greek — Yeshua, meaning "God saves." Even the name of Israel's great leader pointed forward to the Savior who would lead all of God's people into the ultimate Promised Land: eternal life.

🏰 Activity: Walls of Jericho

Build a "wall of Jericho" out of blocks, books, cardboard boxes, or pillows. Then march around it — once, twice... seven times (on the seventh round). Shout and knock it down!

Talk about what "walls" exist in your life — habits, fears, temptations, hard relationships. Read Joshua 1:9 together and ask: what wall does God want to help you knock down this month?
