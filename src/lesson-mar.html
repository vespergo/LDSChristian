Abraham, Isaac, Jacob, Joseph, and Moses — God Keeps His Promises

February 23 – March 29  ·  Genesis 18–50; Exodus 1–6

The Stories This Month

Abraham and Isaac: God asked Abraham to do the hardest possible thing — sacrifice his beloved son Isaac on a mountain. Abraham obeyed, climbing the mountain with a heavy heart. At the last moment, God stopped him and provided a ram instead. It was a test, and Abraham passed it completely. The covenant was renewed: Abraham's family would bless all nations.

Jacob and Esau were twin brothers who wrestled — physically and spiritually — throughout their lives. Jacob deceived his brother, fled, worked fourteen years for the wife he loved, and one night wrestled with an angel until dawn. The angel renamed him "Israel," meaning he had wrestled with God and prevailed. Out of his twelve sons came the twelve tribes of Israel.

Joseph was thrown into a pit by jealous brothers and sold as a slave into Egypt. In prison for years despite doing nothing wrong, he never stopped trusting God. Gradually, through dreams and divine gifts, he rose to become the second most powerful person in Egypt — and used that position to save his entire family, including the very brothers who betrayed him. When he revealed himself and they trembled in fear, he said: "Be not afraid... it was God who sent me here."

Moses was born under a death sentence, hidden in a basket on the Nile, raised in Pharaoh's palace, fled as a fugitive, and found God in a burning bush. God called him back to Egypt to deliver His people. Moses said he couldn't speak well. God said: "I will be with thee."

Abraham's willingness to offer Isaac was the greatest test of faith in the Old Testament — and a powerful symbol of what God would one day do for us.

Let's Talk About It

  • Abraham trusted God even when God asked the most painful thing imaginable. How did that test prepare Abraham for the covenant blessings that followed?
  • Jacob wrestled with an angel all night and would not let go until he received a blessing. What do you "wrestle with" in your life? How can you bring that struggle to God?
  • Joseph was treated horribly by his brothers, yet he forgave them completely and said God had meant it for good. Is there something hard in your life that God might be using for a purpose you can't see yet?
  • Moses said he wasn't a good speaker. God said "I will be with thee." When has God helped you do something you felt you couldn't do?
  • Which of these four stories — Abraham, Jacob, Joseph, Moses — is most meaningful to you right now, and why?

✝ How This Points to Jesus Christ

Abraham's test on that mountain is one of the most powerful symbols in all of scripture. God asked Abraham to sacrifice his only beloved son — and at the last moment, He provided a substitute (the ram). Thousands of years later, God the Father did the same thing for us — but this time, He did not hold back. Jesus Christ was the Lamb God provided. Joseph's story also mirrors Christ's: both were betrayed by those closest to them, sold for silver, falsely condemned, and rose from their lowest point to save the very people who rejected them. Every time God turns someone's suffering into salvation, He is showing us a preview of the Atonement.

🎨 Activity: Joseph's Story in Scenes

Fold a piece of paper into six sections. In each section, draw one scene from Joseph's life:

  1. His coat of many colors
  2. His brothers throwing him in the pit
  3. Joseph in prison
  4. Pharaoh's dream — seven fat cows, seven thin cows
  5. Joseph as ruler of Egypt
  6. Joseph hugging his brothers

Talk about how the whole story was heading somewhere — even the terrible parts. God was writing something bigger than Joseph could see at the time.
