Ruth, Samuel, David, and Solomon — God Looks at the Heart

June 1 – June 28  ·  Ruth; 1 Samuel; 2 Samuel 1–7; 1 Kings 1–11

The Stories This Month

Ruth was not an Israelite — she was from Moab. But when her husband died, she refused to abandon her mother-in-law Naomi. "Where you go, I will go," she said. "Your God will be my God." Ruth chose loyalty over comfort, and God honored her with a new family and a place in the lineage of King David — and of Jesus Christ Himself.

Samuel was a boy sleeping in the temple when he heard a voice call his name in the night. Three times he went to Eli the priest: "Here am I; for thou calledst me." Finally Eli realized: God was speaking to the boy. Samuel learned to say, "Speak, Lord; for thy servant heareth." That willingness to listen is the beginning of prophecy.

David was the youngest of eight brothers — overlooked, left in the fields tending sheep. God said to Samuel: "Man looketh on the outward appearance, but the Lord looketh on the heart." David was anointed king as a boy. He killed Goliath with a sling and stone when the entire army was afraid. He wrote psalms in both victory and heartbreak. He was called "a man after God's own heart" — not because he was perfect, but because when he failed, he truly repented.

Solomon asked God for wisdom — not riches or power — and God gave him all three. He built the magnificent Temple in Jerusalem, fulfilling his father David's greatest dream. Yet in the end, Solomon's heart turned away from God. Even the wisest man in the world struggled with the same weaknesses we all face.

David faced Goliath not with armor but with faith: "The battle is the Lord's." God's power works best through those the world overlooks.

Let's Talk About It

  • Ruth left her home and her people to care for Naomi. When have you put someone else's needs ahead of your own comfort?
  • Samuel heard God's voice as a child. Do you believe God speaks to children? Has He ever spoken to you — through a feeling, a thought, or a moment of peace?
  • God passed over seven of David's older brothers to choose the youngest shepherd boy. What does God look for in a person?
  • David was called "a man after God's own heart" even though he made serious mistakes. What made David's heart different from Saul's?
  • Solomon asked for wisdom, not riches. If God asked you "What do you want?", what would you ask for?

Key Scriptures

Ruth 1:16

"Whither thou goest, I will go; and where thou lodgest, I will lodge: thy people shall be my people, and thy God my God."

1 Samuel 16:7

"For the Lord seeth not as man seeth; for man looketh on the outward appearance, but the Lord looketh on the heart."

1 Samuel 17:47

"And all this assembly shall know that the Lord saveth not with sword and spear: for the battle is the Lord's."

✝ How This Points to Jesus Christ

Ruth's loyalty is a picture of the covenant love — "hesed" — that God has for us: no matter where we have wandered, He says "Where you go, I will go." David was promised that one of his descendants would rule forever (2 Samuel 7:12–13). That promise was fulfilled in Jesus Christ, born in Bethlehem — David's city — whose kingdom has no end. Every time the New Testament calls Jesus "Son of David," it points back to this month's stories. And Solomon's temple, built to house the presence of God, was a symbol of what Jesus accomplished — He made it possible for God to dwell not in a building, but in each of us.

💛 Activity: God Looks at the Heart

Cut a large heart shape from red or pink paper. Inside, write qualities that matter to God — not what people see on the outside, but what's on the inside: kindness, honesty, courage, faith, repentance, love.

Each person takes a turn holding the heart and sharing one quality they want to develop this month. Pray together and ask God to help grow those qualities in each person's heart.
