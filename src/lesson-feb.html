Enoch, Noah, and Abraham — God Calls Ordinary People

January 26 – February 22  ·  Genesis 5; Moses 6–8; Genesis 6–17; Abraham 1–2

The Stories This Month

Enoch felt like nobody — young, slow of speech, hated by people around him. Yet God called him to be a prophet. When he said yes, mountains moved and rivers changed course. Enoch and his people became so righteous that God took their whole city — Zion — up to heaven, and God wept for the rest of the world that chose not to follow.

Noah lived when wickedness filled the earth. God warned him a flood was coming and asked him to build an enormous ark. It took years. People laughed at him. But Noah did "all that God commanded him." When the waters finally receded and Noah's family stepped onto dry ground, God placed a rainbow in the sky — His promise that He would never flood the whole earth again.

Abraham was called to leave his home in Ur and travel to a land he had never seen. God promised him that through his family, all the nations of the earth would be blessed — and that his descendants would be as countless as the stars. That promise, called the Abrahamic covenant, is still in force today. Every person who makes covenants with God becomes part of Abraham's covenant family.

Noah built the ark in faith before a single raindrop fell — a lesson in trusting God's word over other people's opinions.

Let's Talk About It

  • Enoch felt too young and too weak for the job God asked him to do. Have you ever felt that way? What happened?
  • God wept when He saw how many people were suffering and rejecting Him. What does it tell you about God that He weeps?
  • Noah kept building even when no one believed him. What helps you keep going when people don't believe in what you're doing?
  • The rainbow is God's promise. What promises has God made to you? How do you remember them?
  • Abraham left everything to follow God. What is the hardest thing you've ever been asked to give up or change?

✝ How This Points to Jesus Christ

Enoch saw a vision of Christ's suffering and asked: "How is it that the heavens weep and shed forth their tears?" God answered that He wept because His children were suffering and choosing evil. That same love drove God to send His Son. Noah's ark is a powerful symbol of Jesus — just as only those inside the ark were saved from the flood, only those who come to Christ are saved from spiritual death. And Abraham's covenant — that all nations would be blessed through his family — was ultimately fulfilled when Jesus Christ, a descendant of Abraham, came to earth to bless the whole world.

🌈 Activity: God's Promises

Draw a rainbow on a large piece of paper. In each color band, write one of God's promises you know or have learned about — promises about families, forgiveness, resurrection, covenants, or guidance.

Then go outside (or look at a photo of the night sky) and try to count the stars. Remember God's promise to Abraham: his family — the covenant family — would be that numerous. If you have been baptized, you are part of that family right now.
